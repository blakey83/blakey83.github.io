Personal Profile



Briggs Meys test




My Briggs Meyers test results revealed that I’m an INTJ-A, an Assertive Architect. This means, for me,
that I can use rational thought and ingenuity to bring a project to fruition.

In a team environment, my abilities to build and solve problems would be an asset. I have a high work
ethic, and believe in my abilities. However I’m not the most able to accommodate the feelings of others.

When forming a group to work on a project, I would certainly be a positive member of the group. However,
my limitations would mean that I would need someone who has a strong character with a lot of empathy to
hold me to account when I’m dealing with other members of the team.





Learning Styles Test




I found a learning styles test online at http://www.educationplanner.org.The results of the test say that
I’m a 50% tactile learner. To be honest, this surprised me, as I’ve always been able to decipher things
through reading books, which has been especially helpful in my career as an Electronics Technician. However I do
believe this is helpful, as I can change how I process and learn information, and will incorporate some “doing”
things to re-enforce my study

What this will mean for my behaviour inside a team, it would mean that we would possibly need to have more meetings,
but make them shorter. I also think that I would need to take on task that have more of a physical component, as I would
be more successful at these tasks. I also believe that it would benefit me to hold more face to face meetings, than to
only have contact online

So in regards to forming a group, I think that it would be beneficial to find group members that are in my geographical
location. Although this may not be possible due to the fact that I am studying distance education. It was also be important
to find team members that compliment my learning styles. For example, if I was able to find someone to partner up with that
is more of a visual learner, then we could share the load in such a way, that the heavier reading could be done by them,
and the more practical work could be done by myself.





Productivity Test




The third test that I found online was to assess my productivity, it was conducted at https://hbr.org/2018/08/assessment-how-productive-are-you.
The results show that I am 95% productive. This is a good results, and I think it shows that my choices over the last number
of years have paid off, and I do feel more productive than I have in any other time in my life. However, there is always room
for improvement. While answering the questions, I did consider more ways that I could improve my productivity

In a group environment, obviously, being a productive member is incredibly important. Being able to be relied on by my team
mates is something that I would pride myself on. Hopefully this level of productivity is emulated by my team. I think that
organizing communications, and setting task efficiently and productively would make success a lot easier.

When forming a team, I think it would be better to find people who are also organized and productive. I think trying to maintain
a high level of organization with someone who works in an ad-hoc manner would be frustrating, and would lead to friction within the group.
